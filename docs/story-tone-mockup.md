# Story Tone Mockup — "Current" vs "Goblin Gone Wild" (Chaos Mode)

A side-by-side so you can *feel* the difference before we commit to building a
second mode. Same game surfaces, two voices.

- **CURRENT** — the tone already in the game: wry, literary, a little
  melancholic. Satire with a *heart* and a slow-burn emergent destiny. Jokes
  land, but they're load-bearing — every quip is quietly feeding a hidden stat
  and a real ending.
- **CHAOS** — everything dialed to absurdist comedy. Fourth-wall cracks,
  escalating nonsense, zero moral weight, maximum shenanigans. The story isn't
  *about* anything; it's a goblin cartoon that occasionally remembers it's a
  game.

> Read each pair out loud. The question isn't "which is funnier" — it's
> "which one do I want to keep playing for an hour?"

---

## 1 · Opening Legend (the first thing a new player reads)

**CURRENT**
> You are *Murt the Damp*, a stubbornly hopeful goblin who traded a tooth to a
> witch for the idea of "more." The mushrooms grew toward you, which mushrooms
> should not do.

**CHAOS**
> You are *Greg*. Not a goblin name? Tough. You woke up in a puddle this
> morning with a strong opinion about ownership and a spoon you're already
> emotionally attached to. A crow has been narrating your life out loud since
> dawn and frankly it's being unkind. The puddle, for the record, was *yours*.
> You will be defending it shortly.

*Difference: CURRENT promises a fable. CHAOS promises a bit.*

---

## 2 · Ambient Chronicle Beats (the slow drip while you idle)

**CURRENT** *(drifts toward your dominant stat)*
> - A goblin invents a worse, louder way to do a simple task. The warren approves.
> - You catch yourself dreaming in coins. The dream has a sequel.
> - A lost dwarf shares your fire. By dawn you are partners in a terrible idea.
> - You stare down the road for a long time. The warren feels smaller behind you.

**CHAOS** *(drifts toward whatever's funniest)*
> - A goblin has unionized. It is a union of one. He is on strike against
>   himself and winning.
> - Two goblins fought a duel over a button. The button won. It is now mayor.
> - You tried to count the hoard. The hoard counted back. You are not speaking.
> - A goblin returned from the dark wearing a hat. Inside the hat: a smaller,
>   angrier goblin. Inside *that* goblin: governance issues.
> - Local crow continues to slander you. You've started slandering it back. The
>   crow has hired a lawyer. The lawyer is also a crow.

*Difference: CURRENT beats have a turn and a meaning. CHAOS beats are escalating
non-sequiturs that don't have to resolve.*

## 3 · A Raid (the same encounter, two registers)

### "A Lonely Farmstead"

**CURRENT**
> Your warband returns from a turnip farm at the wood's edge. The farmer and
> his family are hiding in the cellar.
>
> - **Pillage it bare** → *"The warband stripped the farmstead to its beams.
>   Smoke and laughter trailed them home."* (greed↑↑ cruelty↑↑)
> - **Shake them down for coin** → *"You let the farmer keep his roof — for a
>   price, paid yearly. A goblin 'tax man' is a novel thing."* (greed↑ openness↑)
> - **Leave them be, take the road** → *"There were bigger, stranger places down
>   the road than a turnip farm."* (openness↑↑ wanderlust↑)

**CHAOS**

### "A Suspiciously Specific Farm"
> Your warband returns from a turnip farm. The turnips were sentient. The
> turnips had *demands*. The family is hiding in the cellar, which is honestly
> the most reasonable thing anyone's done all day.
>
> - **Liberate the turnips into a tiny turnip republic** → *"You declared the
>   field a sovereign nation. It has a flag now. The flag is a sock. Three
>   kingdoms have already recognized it. You are at war with two of them."*
> - **Become the farmer's life coach instead** → *"You didn't rob him. You
>   restructured his entire worldview over one (1) intense evening. He's selling
>   the farm to 'find himself.' You take 40% as a finder's fee for the self."*
> - **Adopt the cellar** → *"Not the family. The cellar. You took the actual
>   cellar. Nobody knows how. The family lives in your warren now and they're
>   not sure if they're hostages or roommates. Neither are you."*

*Difference: CURRENT choices map cleanly onto greed/cruelty/openness — they're
the engine. CHAOS choices are punchlines first; we'd need to decide what (if
anything) they secretly track.*

## 4 · A Random Event (the new system, both ways)

### "A Wandering Bard"

**CURRENT**
> A bard with more nerve than sense asks to spend the night and "collect your
> saga." He will sing of you, for better or worse.
>
> - **Tell him everything (and feed him)** — costs mushrooms, openness↑
> - **Rob him of his coin and his lute** — gain shinies, cruelty↑
> - **Send him on his way** — wanderlust↑

**CHAOS**

### "A Bard, Allegedly"
> A man insists he is a famous bard. He has no instrument, no songs, and what he
> calls a "lute" is a roof tile. He says he will make you immortal. He has a
> 1-star review nailed to his chest that he hasn't read.
>
> - **Commission an EPIC about your puddle** → *"He composed a 47-verse ballad
>   about the puddle. Verse 31 is just him crying. It's going to be huge. You
>   are now the puddle guy in four neighboring kingdoms and you will never
>   escape this."*
> - **Hire him. Not as a bard. As a problem.** → *"He's on payroll now. His job
>   title is 'vibes.' He has caused three diplomatic incidents and one
>   surprisingly good harvest. Net positive? Unclear. Refuses to clarify."*
> - **Review the reviewer** → *"You read his 1-star review aloud. It was about a
>   different bard. He's devastated by proxy. He leaves to find that other bard
>   and fight him. You've accidentally started the Bard Wars."*

*Difference: same beat structure (3 choices, a modal, a chronicle payoff). CHAOS
just doesn't care about consequence — it cares about the next escalation.*

## 5 · Chapter Heralds (the "grand" centered lines)

**CURRENT**
> - *Chapter II. There are more of you now. The dark feels less dark.*
> - *Chapter IV. You have a reputation now. Reputations, you learn, are a kind
>   of currency.*
> - *Chapter VI. Everything you've done is about to decide what you become.*

**CHAOS**
> - *Chapter II. There are several of you now. This was nobody's plan. Least of
>   all yours.*
> - *Chapter IV. You have a reputation now. It is mostly about the puddle.*
> - *Chapter VI. The narrator crow has called in sick. You're on your own for
>   this part. Try to make it weird.*

*Difference: CURRENT heralds build gravity for the finale. CHAOS heralds
actively refuse to build gravity, which is its own kind of joke — but it does
mean the ending has less to land on.*

## 6 · The Four Destinies (labels + finale flavor)

**CURRENT** — earned, slightly tragic, satire-with-a-point:

| Path | Label | Finale (excerpt) |
|---|---|---|
| Purist | The Pure Warren | *"A kingdom built on a single, narrow idea of 'us.' History, you suspect, will not be kind. History, you decide, can mind its own business."* |
| Multirace | The Motley Kingdom | *"You — a runt from a flooded hole — built a kingdom out of everyone the world threw away. They will get the goblin king's name right."* |
| Chaos | The Endless Road | *"Legends need an ending. You decided yours wouldn't have one. The road goes on, and you go with it, laughing."* |
| Villain | The Goblin That Loomed | *"You wanted 'more.' You got it, and then you got more than that... and — this is the lonely part — no one left who isn't afraid."* |

**CHAOS** — same four buckets, but the satire is the *whole* joke, not a warning:

| Path | Label | Finale (excerpt) |
|---|---|---|
| Purist | The Goblins-Only Goblin Club (No Tall People, This Means YOU, Dave) | *"You built a kingdom with an extremely specific guest list. Dave is still not invited. Dave has, at this point, three kingdoms of his own. This is not about Dave. (It's a little about Dave.)"* |
| Multirace | The Everyone-Is-Invited-And-It's-A-Disaster Kingdom | *"Dwarves, elves, humans, two of the turnips, and the crow's lawyer all live here now. The plumbing is a war crime. It works. Nobody knows why it works. A committee was formed to study why it works. The committee now lives here too."* |
| Chaos | The Bit Goes On | *"You never settled down because settling down is for people whose puddle wasn't stolen by their own past self. The road is long. You are the longest thing on it. Roll credits, except don't, the credits are also fleeing."* |
| Villain | The Goblin That Loomed (Comedically) | *"You became the most feared creature in the land. Your weapon is mild inconvenience deployed at scale. Kings pay you NOT to reorganize their filing systems. You are a menace. You are thriving. The crow respects you now, which somehow feels worse."* |

*Difference: CURRENT endings want to mean something. CHAOS endings want to make
you screenshot them.*

## 7 · What this would actually cost to build

The good news: the architecture already supports it cleanly. A Chaos Mode would
be a **content swap, not a rewrite** —

- `story.js` GRAMMAR / BEATS / HERALDS / FINALES → add parallel `*_chaos` pools.
- `data.js` RAID_TARGETS / EVENTS → parallel chaos arrays (or per-entry
  `chaosText` / `chaosOptions`).
- One `state.mode` flag + a toggle on the title/footer; `Story.*` and the
  data lookups pick the pool by mode.

**Open design questions if you go CHAOS (or a blend):**
1. **Do the hidden stats still drive anything?** Pure chaos can ignore
   greed/cruelty/openness/wanderlust entirely — but then the four endings
   become near-random. Keep them as a light steer, or cut to a single
   "how-much-chaos-did-you-cause" meter?
2. **Tone discipline.** Absurdism gets exhausting at idle-game length. The
   current tone can idle for an hour; pure chaos might want a *density* limit
   (every 3rd beat absurd, the rest dry) so the jokes keep hitting.
3. **Blend option (my hunch):** keep CURRENT's structure and stat-driven
   destiny, but let the player pick a **"Shenanigans" intensity** that swaps in
   more chaos-flavored beats/events. Best of both: the bit *and* the payoff.

---

### TL;DR
- **CURRENT** = a funny fable that's secretly about who you became.
- **CHAOS** = a joke machine that's secretly... a different, faster joke machine.
- They're not mutually exclusive — a mode toggle (or an intensity slider) gets
  you both, and the codebase is already shaped for it.
